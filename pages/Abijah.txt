'''abijah''' ({{lang-he|אֲבִיָּה}} ''’ăḇîyāh''; also ''abiah'', ''[[abia (name)|abia]]''; in [[modern hebrew]] ''aviya'') is a [[biblical hebrew]]<ref name="petrovsky">petrovsky, p.&nbsp;35</ref> [[unisex name]]<ref name="superanskaya2">superanskaya, p.&nbsp;277</ref> that means "my father is [[yahweh|yah]]".<ref name="petrovsky">petrovsky, p.&nbsp;35</ref><ref>the hebrew form ''aviyahu'' also occurs in the bible. see w. e. addis and t. k. cheyne (1899) "abijah" in cheyne and black, eds., ''encyclopaedia biblica.'' [https://archive.org/details/encyclopaediabib01cheyuoft]</ref>  ==russian name== the variant used in the [[russian language]] is "{{lang|ru|а́вия}}" (''aviya''),<ref name="petrovsky" /> with "{{lang|ru|а́бия}}"<ref name="petrovsky" /> or "{{lang|ru|аби́я}}" (''abiya''),<ref name="superanskaya2" /> being older forms.<ref name="petrovsky" /> included into various, often handwritten, church calendars throughout the 17th–19th centuries, it was omitted from the official [[synod]]al [[menologium]] at the end of the 19th century.<ref name="superanskaya2a">superanskaya, pp.&nbsp;22, 23, and 277</ref> in 1924–1930, the name (as "{{lang|ru|ави́я}}", a form of "abiya"<ref name="superanskaya2" />) was included into various soviet calendars,<ref name="superanskaya2a" /> which included the new and often artificially created names promoting the new soviet realities and encouraging the break with the tradition of using the names in the synodal menologia.<ref name="dushechkina">toronto slavic quarterly. елена душечкина. "[http://sites.utoronto.ca/tsq/12/dushechkina12.shtml мессианские тенденции в советской антропонимической практике 1920-х - 1930-х годов]" {{ru icon}}</ref> in russian it is only used as a female name.<ref name="petrovsky" /><ref name="superanskaya2" /> [[diminutive]]s of this name include "{{lang|ru|а́ва}}" (''ava'') and "{{lang|ru|ви́я}}" (''viya'').<ref name="petrovsky">petrovsky, p.&nbsp;35</ref>  ==old testament characters== ===women=== *[[abijah (queen)]], ([[2 chronicles]] 29:1), who married king [[ahaz]] of judah. she is also called abi. (2 [[books of kings|kings]] 18:2) her father's name was zechariah; she was the mother of king [[hezekiah]] ({{bibleverse|2|chronicles|29:1}}). *a wife of [[hezron|hetzron]], one of the grandchildren of [[judah (son of jacob)|judah]] (1 chronicles 2:24).  ===men=== *[[abijah of judah]], also known as abijam (אבים '' 'aḅiyam'' "my father is yam [sea]"), who was son of [[rehoboam]] and succeeded him on the throne of [[kingdom of judah|judah]] (1 chronicles 3:10, [[matthew 1:7]], 1 kings 14:31). *a son of [[becher (biblical figure)|becher]], the son of [[benjamin]] (1 chr. 7:8). *the second son of [[samuel]] ([[1 samuel]] 8:2; 1 chronicles 6:28). his conduct, along with that of his brother, as a judge in [[beersheba]], to which office his father had appointed him, led to popular discontent, and ultimately provoked the people to demand a [[monarchy]]. *a descendant of [[eleazar]], the son of [[aaron]], a chief of one of the twenty-four orders into which the priesthood was divided by [[david]] (1 chr. 24:10). the order of abijah is listed with the priests and levites who returned with zerubbabel son of shealtiel and with joshua. [[book of nehemiah|nehemiah]] 12:17; 12:1). *a son of [[jeroboam]], the first king of israel. on account of his severe illness when a youth, his father sent [[wife of jeroboam|his wife]] to consult the prophet [[ahijah the shilonite|ahijah]] regarding his recovery. the prophet, though blind with old age, knew the wife of jeroboam as soon as she approached, and under a divine impulse he announced to her that inasmuch as in abijah alone of all the house of jeroboam there was found "some good thing toward the lord", he only would come to his grave in peace. as his mother crossed the threshold of the door on her return, the youth died, and "all israel mourned for him" ([[1 kings]] 14:1–18). *the head of the eighth of the twenty-four courses into which [[david]] divided the priests, and an ancestor of [[zechariah (priest)|zecharias]] the priest, who was the father of [[john the baptist]] ({{bibleref|1|chronicles|24:10|kjv}}, {{bibleref|luke|1:5|kjv}}, {{bibleref|luke|1:13|kjv}}).  ==references== ===notes=== {{reflist}}  ===sources=== *н. а. петровский (n. a. petrovsky).
