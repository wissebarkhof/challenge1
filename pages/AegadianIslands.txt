{{unreferenced|date=may 2011}} [[file:aegadian islands map.png|thumb|a map showing the aegadian islands]] [[file:mare favignana.jpg|thumb|cala rossa, favignana]]  the '''aegadian islands ''' ({{lang-it|isole egadi}}; [[sicilian language|sicilian]]: ''ìsuli ègadi'', {{lang-la|aegates insulae}}, {{lang-el|αιγάται νήσοι}}, meaning "the islands of goats") are a group of five small mountainous islands in the [[mediterranean sea]] off the northwest coast of [[sicily]], [[italy]], near the cities of [[trapani]] and [[marsala]], with a total area of {{convert|37.45|km2|sqmi|2}}.  the [[favignana#island|island of favignana]] (''aegusa''), the largest, lies {{convert|16|km|mi}} southwest of trapani; [[levanzo]] (''phorbantia'') lies {{convert|13|km|mi}} west; and [[marettimo]], the ancient ''iera nesos'', {{convert|24|km|mi}} west of trapani, is now reckoned as a part of the group. there are also two minor islands, formica and maraone, lying between levanzo and sicily. for administrative purposes the archipelago constitutes the [[favignana|comune of favignana]] in the [[province of trapani]].  the overall population in 1987 was estimated at about 5,000. winter frost is unknown and rainfall is low. the main occupation of the islanders is fishing, and the largest [[tuna]] fishery in sicily is here.  [[file:favignana cala azzurra.jpg|thumb|right|cala azzurra, favignana]] [[file:erice-views-bjs-2.jpg|thumb|left|a view from [[erice]] to favignana and levanzo. on the horizon marettimo is faintly visible.]]  ==history== there is evidence of [[neolithic]] and even [[paleolithic]] paintings in caves on levanzo, and to a lesser extent on favignana.  the islands were the scene of the [[battle of the aegates islands]] of 241 bc, in which the [[carthage|carthaginian]] fleet was defeated by the roman fleet led by [[gaius lutatius catulus|c. lutatius catulus]]; the engagement ended the [[first punic war]]. after the end of [[western roman empire|western roman]] power in the first millennium ad, the islands, to the extent that they were governed at all, were part of territories of [[goths]], [[vandals]], [[saracens]], before the [[normans]] fortified favignana in 1081.  the islands belonged to the pallavicini-rusconi family of [[genoa]] until 1874, when the [[florio]] family of [[palermo]] bought them.  {{coord|37|58|n|12|12|e|region:it-tp_type:isle_source:dewiki|display=title}}  ==references== {{reflist}}  ==external links== {{wikivoyage|aegadian islands}} * {{dmoz|world/italiano/regionale/europa/italia/sicilia/provincia_di_trapani/isole_egadi/}}  {{regions of the world}}  [[category:aegadian islands]] [[category:archipelagoes of italy]]   {{sicily-geo-stub}}
